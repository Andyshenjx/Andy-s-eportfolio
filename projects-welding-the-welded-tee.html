Project

Welding The Welded Tee

Practicing tee-joint welding with SMAW and GMAW, then completing finished cap passes for both processes.

Goal

Goal

  • Learn how to weld a tee joint using both SMAW and GMAW.
  • Practice bead control, travel speed, and weld consistency.
  • Complete finished cap pass examples with each welding process.

Tools Used

Tools Used

  • SMAW Welder
  • GMAW Welder
  • Electrodes and Welding Wire
  • Steel Tee Joint Practice Pieces
  • Welding Helmet, Gloves, and Protective Clothing
  • Wire Brush and Cleanup Tools

What I Did

What I Did

  • Prepared tee-joint pieces for welding practice.
  • Ran multiple practice welds to improve bead shape and control.
  • Worked through both SMAW and GMAW setups to compare the two processes.
  • Focused on building cleaner, more even passes before the final cap pass.
  • Finished the project with one completed cap pass sample for each method.

Safety & Reflection

Safety & Reflection

  • Wore a welding helmet, gloves, and protective clothing during all welding work.
  • Kept the workspace clear and stayed aware of sparks and hot metal.
  • Handled the finished pieces carefully because the metal stayed hot after welding.
  • Followed proper setup and shutdown steps for both welding machines.
  • I learned how SMAW and GMAW feel different when controlling the weld puddle.
  • I practiced keeping a more even bead by adjusting my speed and position.
  • I improved my confidence with tee-joint welding by repeating practice passes.
  • This project showed me that strong welds come from patience, control, and repetition.
